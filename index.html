Roe v. Wade in the Nebraska Legislature

Tracking how abortion restrictions moved through Nebraska's Unicameral in 2023, focusing on LB626 and how its provisions later appeared in LB574.

Title Page

What is this about?

After the US Supreme Court overturned Roe v. Wade, Nebraska lawmakers debated new abortion restrictions. This project focuses on how Nebraska legislators handled a "heartbeat" bill (LB626) and how its abortion restrictions later appeared in another bill (LB574).

Bill explained on the title page

LB626, introduced January 17, 2023, was titled "Adopt the Nebraska Heartbeat Act." It proposed restricting abortions once "cardiac activity" could be detected, commonly described as around six weeks of pregnancy. LB626 did not become law on its own, later portions were amended into LB574.

Source: Nebraska Legislature bill pages for LB626 and LB574.

Significance

Purpose and what it changed or proposed

The significance of these bills was that they would change Nebraska's abortion policy after Roe v. Wade. LB626 proposed an earlier restriction (heartbeat standard). LB574 ultimately became law, including an abortion restriction based on weeks of pregnancy, plus other health related provisions.
